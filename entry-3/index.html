Beef Wellington

Rich, classic, and comforting.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ pounds beef tenderloin
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened, divided
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • ½ cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 2 ounces liver paté
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (17.5 oz) package puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 large egg yolk, beaten
  • 1 (10.5 oz) can beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons red wine

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Place beef tenderloin in a baking dish; spread 2 tbsp softened butter over beef.
  3. Bake 10–15 minutes until browned; reserve juices and cool completely.
  4. Increase oven to 450°F (230°C).
  5. Sauté onion + mushrooms in butter for 5 minutes; cool.
  6. Mix paté + remaining butter, season, spread over beef; add mushroom mix.
  7. Roll puff pastry to ¼ inch thick; place beef in center.
  8. Fold pastry, seal edges, cut slits, brush with egg yolk.
  9. Bake 10 min, lower to 425°F, bake 10–15 more until golden.
  10. Boil pan juices with broth + wine 5 minutes; strain and serve.

Entree. Beef Wellington

Rich, classic, and comforting.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ pounds beef tenderloin
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened, divided
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • ½ cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 2 ounces liver paté
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (17.5 ounce) package frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 large egg yolk, beaten
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons red wine

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Place beef tenderloin in a baking dish; spread 2 tablespoons softened butter over beef.
  3. Bake 10 to 15 minutes until browned. Reserve juices and cool beef.
  4. Increase oven to 450°F (230°C).
  5. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a skillet; sauté onion and mushrooms 5 minutes, cool.
  6. Mix paté and remaining butter; season and spread over beef, add mushroom mix.
  7. Roll out puff pastry to ¼" thick and place beef in center.
  8. Fold pastry, seal edges, cut a few slits, brush with egg yolk.
  9. Bake 10 minutes at 450°F, reduce to 425°F and bake 10 to 15 more until golden (122 to 130°F for medium-rare). Rest.
  10. Boil reserved pan juices with beef broth + red wine ~5 minutes, strain, serve.
